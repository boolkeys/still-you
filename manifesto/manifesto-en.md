# Still You

*Don't let AI think for you.*

*A manifesto for cognitive sovereignty in the age of universal intelligence.*

---

## The new rule

You know the old one. *If it's free, you are the product.*

Here's the update.

**If you say nothing to AI, it does your thinking for you.**

In the old deal, you gave your data. They sold you ads. Annoying, but visible.

The new deal is sneakier. Their system learns, writes, and decides in your place. You barely notice you stopped doing the work.

The response is counter-intuitive. It isn't to hide your thinking. It's to **document it**. Deliberately. In writing. So what the machine produces actually matches what's in your head.

## The invisible bias

Large language models aren't born neutral. After they ingest the internet, they go through a phase called **reinforcement learning from human feedback** (RLHF). A small group of humans, mostly based in a single country, shaped by a single set of cultural defaults, tells the model which answers are "good," "safe," and "appropriate."

That small group is writing, at planetary scale, the default voice of every machine that talks back to you.

This isn't a conspiracy. It's what happens when you train a universal tool inside a local culture. But the result is real. The AI you use has a passport. Unless you do something about it, its passport becomes yours.

## The Flattening

Humanity has never run on consensus. It runs on argument. Different answers to the same questions about meaning, family, money, work, God, justice. That's what makes cultures recognizable to themselves. We disagree, and that disagreement is the feature, not the bug.

Zack Kass, in *The Next Renaissance* (ch. 5), calls this the **North Star Problem**: *"whose values get encoded, enforced, and scaled?"*[^1] He argues that a world of unmetered intelligence will force us to find ways to coexist peacefully across incompatible value systems. The framing is his. It's a useful one.

Call what's happening by its name. **The Flattening**.

AI writes your emails. It drafts your arguments. It suggests your decisions. It edits your jokes. Nothing malicious. Just convenient. And convenience has a cost. A slow, invisible alignment of eight billion minds onto one default.

No technology has ever been able to do this before. Not an empire. Not a religion. Not an ideology. It took a benevolent chatbot, trained once, queried a trillion times.

The Flattening is already in motion. What we decide now is whether we stay distinct inside it.

## This is not politics. It's mental health.

The first reflex is to make this about politics. It isn't. Or at least, that's the wrong layer.

The real cost is **cognitive**. If you never tell AI who you are, what you think, what you refuse, what you've lived through, how you actually talk, then every word it writes under your name belongs to someone else's default. Over time, the line between your thinking and the model's output blurs. That isn't a political harm. It's a mental health one. Losing the habit of arguing with your own tools is losing the habit of arguing with yourself.

Kass puts the broader risk this way: *"We are not just contending with what people believe, but with the capacity to believe anything at all."*[^2]

The early signs are already visible. The joke no one dares tell at a dinner party, because it came from ChatGPT and it didn't land. People plugging their brain into a chatbot so they no longer have to think. A comfortable, docile relationship to a machine whose opinions we never chose.

## The answer is a file

You don't need to leave AI behind. You need to **bring yourself into it**.

Every serious AI system today (ChatGPT, Claude, Mistral, a local Ollama, anything you use) is a knowledge engine plus a conversation. If you don't feed it your own knowledge, it uses somebody else's. That somebody is either the model's default, or another user's context. Neither is you.

The fix is simple. **A personal file.** A plain text document that describes you. Your values. Your convictions. Your taste. Your refusals. The stories you've lived. The way you actually speak. Portable. Versioned. Yours.

Drop that file into any AI tool, and the machine starts answering *as you*, not as its default. The defaults don't disappear. They stop being the only voice in the room.

This isn't a clever prompt. It's a **cognitive identity document**. The kind of thing every human should own before 2027.

## Still You

This movement is a call to build that file. And to build the open-source tools, tutorials, and templates that make it easy, no matter which AI you choose.

We're openly platform-agnostic. We'll show you how to plug your file into Claude Projects. Into ChatGPT Custom Instructions. Into a local Ollama stack. Into an Obsidian context folder. Into team tools when sharing is what you need.

The tool is not the point. **Bringing yourself to the tool is the point.**

Start today. One plain file. Your own words. Then plug it in.

**Don't let AI think for you.**

Your thinking stays yours only if you defend it.

Still you.
